## "Your Wish Is My Command" System Brings Azure, GitHub, AI Orchestration, And Notary Geek Operations Together

GoodWare LLC announces the private release of **Wish Upon A Star**, a new internal automation feature designed to turn structured requests, operational needs, and human intent into actionable technical workflows.

The feature is currently released privately, with [Notary Geek](https://notarygeek.net/) serving as an early consumer of the technology. Built around Microsoft Azure, GitHub, automation queues, and AI-assisted execution, Wish Upon A Star is part of GoodWare LLC's broader effort to make practical technology respond more directly to real human needs.

The core idea is simple: within technical, legal, security, privacy, and operational limits, a person should be able to describe what they need and have the system help route, process, document, and complete the work.

GoodWare LLC is building this capability with a vendor-neutral approach to artificial intelligence. The system is intended to work with the broader AI ecosystem, including technologies and models from OpenAI, Microsoft, Anthropic, Google, Meta, xAI, Amazon Web Services, Mistral AI, Cohere, Perplexity, DeepSeek, Alibaba/Qwen, Baidu, IBM, NVIDIA, Hugging Face, Groq, Together AI, Cerebras, Stability AI, Runway, ElevenLabs, Replicate, Cloudflare, and other current and emerging AI providers.

This statement is not intended to claim a formal partnership, endorsement, certification, or integration with each named provider. It describes a vendor-neutral design posture: GoodWare LLC expects useful automation systems to meet people where the best available tools are, while preserving human review, security boundaries, and operational judgment.

"Wish Upon A Star is about making technology feel less like a maze and more like a command surface," said Greg Lirette of GoodWare LLC. "Notary Geek gives us a real-world operating environment where we can test useful automation against actual business needs. The goal is not AI for show. The goal is to help people get things done."

The private release focuses on internal and controlled workflows first, including task routing, GitHub issue tracking, Azure-backed execution, durable documentation, and AI-assisted review. GoodWare LLC expects the system to continue evolving as a practical orchestration layer for business operations, public knowledge work, technical automation, and future customer-facing tools.
